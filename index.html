The Origin Story

A cancer diagnosis he called a gift.

In 2015, Sydney entrepreneur Ali Banat was diagnosed with stage-4 cancer and given months to live. Rather than despair, he sold his cars, his businesses, his designer life — and spent his remaining time building an institution that would outlive him.

2015

2015 — The Pivot

Ali Banat, a successful young Australian entrepreneur, is diagnosed with stage-4 cancer. He calls it "a gift" — the push he needed to leave behind wealth and luxury for a life of purpose.

2015

2015 — Togo, Africa

He sells his cars, clothes and companies, flies to Togo, and founds MATW Project — Muslims Around The World — dedicating the rest of his life to charity on the ground.

2018

2018 — The Legacy Continues

After Ali's passing, his close friend Mahmoud Ismail takes over as CEO, carrying the mission forward and scaling it into one of the most recognized faith-based charities in Australia, the UK, the US and Canada.

Today

Today — 10 Years, 19M+ Lives Touched

Orphan villages in Togo, a growing orphanage and medical complex in Lebanon, solar water wells across Gaza and Togo, and daily emergency relief in Gaza, Sudan, Yemen, Syria and Afghanistan.

Global Impact

Where your Sadaqah actually goes.

Every project is chosen for continuous, compounding impact — Sadaqah Jariyah that keeps giving long after the donation is made.

Orphan & Widow Care

Supporting 18,768 orphans and 264 widows across 8 countries — Togo, Lebanon, Palestine, Jordan, Syria, Yemen, Afghanistan and Bangladesh — plus education for over 5,000 children.

Water Aid & Infrastructure

Daily water tank deliveries in Sudan and Gaza, plus deep solar-powered wells and hand pumps across Togo, Bangladesh and Uganda — built to break the cycle of waterborne illness for good.

Emergency Crisis Relief

Rapid deployment of hot meals, baby milk, diapers and medical kits directly into Gaza and Sudan — thousands of families reached every single week.
